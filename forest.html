Forests in India

India's diverse forests encompass tropical rainforests in the Western Ghats, the dense forests of the Sundarbans mangrove delta, and the rich biodiversity of Jim Corbett and Ranthambore National Parks, offering habitats for myriad species and eco-tourism opportunities.

Western Ghats

The Western Ghats of India harbor lush tropical rainforests teeming with biodiversity, including endemic flora and fauna. These vital ecosystems provide essential ecological services and support local communities while offering breathtaking landscapes for exploration and conservation efforts.

Sundarbans

The Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a vast mangrove forest spanning India and Bangladesh, famed for its Royal Bengal Tigers and unique ecosystem. It's a critical habitat, protecting coastlines, biodiversity, and supporting local communities.

National Parks

India's national parks feature diverse forest ecosystems, from the dense forests of Jim Corbett and Ranthambore to the tropical jungles of Periyar and Kaziranga, harboring iconic wildlife like tigers, elephants, and rhinoceros, preserving India's natural heritage.
