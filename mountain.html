Mountains in India

India boasts a diverse range of mountains, including the mighty Himalayas in the north, the scenic Western Ghats in the west, and the picturesque Eastern Ghats in the east, offering breathtaking landscapes and rich biodiversity.

Himalayas

The Himalayas, spanning India's northern border, stand as the world's highest mountain range, with peaks like Everest and K2. Their snow-capped majesty not only shapes India's geography but also hosts diverse ecosystems and cultures, attracting adventurers and spiritual seekers alike.

Aravalli Range

The Aravalli Range, stretching across northwest India, is one of the oldest mountain ranges globally. Known for its rugged terrain and diverse flora and fauna, it serves as a vital watershed and offers stunning landscapes for exploration.

Western Ghats

The Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, stretch along the western coast of India, harboring rich biodiversity and unique ecosystems. They feature lush forests, cascading waterfalls, and diverse wildlife, making them a haven for nature lovers and adventurers alike.

Eastern Ghats

The Eastern Ghats of India, running parallel to the eastern coast, are a series of low to moderate mountain ranges with scenic beauty, diverse flora and fauna, and cultural significance, offering opportunities for trekking, wildlife exploration, and cultural immersion.
